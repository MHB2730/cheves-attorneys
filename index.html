What we do

A focused practice, deeply held.

Cheves Attorneys does a small number of things exceptionally well — each drawing on nearly two decades at the intersection of environmental science, land use and administrative law.

Environmental Law

Interpretation of environmental legislation, environmental authorisations, compliance strategy, and licences and permits under NEMA and its regulations.

Planning Law

Municipal planning objections and appeals, rezonings, subdivisions and development approvals under SPLUMA and provincial planning frameworks.

Municipal Rates

Property valuation and rating disputes under the Local Government: Municipal Property Rates Act, including objections and valuation appeals.

Filling Station Development

Site and retail licence applications for petroleum filling stations under the Petroleum Products Act — for applicants and objectors alike.

National Building Regulations

Building plan approvals and compliance under the National Building Regulations and Building Standards Act.

Property & Land Survey

Related property matters (excluding conveyancing) and land survey legislation, where they touch environmental, planning and development questions.

About the firm

Nearly two decades at the frontier of environmental law.

Bob Cheves opened his niche firm in March 2024 after almost twenty years as a partner at Norman Brauteseth & Associates Attorneys, which he joined in 2004. The practice was built around a single conviction: that environmental, planning and development law deserves specialists, not generalists.

He holds BA and LLB degrees, and a Master's in Environmental Law obtained with distinction. Beyond the office, his life is shaped by the same landscapes his work protects — running, birdwatching, indigenous gardening, camping and amateur photography.
